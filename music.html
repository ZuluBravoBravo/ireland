Traditional Irish music has ancient roots, dating back thousands of years, deeply intertwined with Ireland’s cultural and social history. It evolved through oral tradition, featuring distinctive instruments like the fiddle, tin whistle, bodhrán, and uilleann pipes. For centuries, this music accompanied communal events, storytelling, dances, and rituals, preserving Ireland’s language and identity despite periods of political hardship and cultural suppression. The 20th century saw a revival movement that brought traditional Irish music to international audiences, helping to keep the vibrant melodies and rhythms alive today in pubs, festivals, and modern compositions worldwide.
